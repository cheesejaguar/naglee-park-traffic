Interventions Timeline

Key milestones in Naglee Park and San Jose traffic safety from 1972 to 2026.

1972

I-280 opens through central San Jose, reshaping Naglee Park's southern traffic patterns.

1983

Campus Community Association funds its own traffic study after the city refuses.

Late 1980s

43 concrete medians installed throughout the neighborhood. Crashes drop from 47 to 27 in comparable periods (43% reduction).

1990s

Speed humps added on multiple residential streets, forming layered traffic-calming network.

2000

Council Policy 5-6 formalizes traffic calming framework for residential neighborhoods.

2015

San Jose adopts Vision Zero — 4th U.S. city to commit to eliminating traffic deaths.

2018–19

Better Bikeways Phase I: 10 miles of protected bike lanes downtown including San Fernando St, 10th & 11th St. Cost: $1.5M.

2020–21

Senter Road quick-build: KSI crashes drop from 15 to 5 — 67% reduction, under $1M.

2022

65 traffic deaths — modern San Jose record. 32 pedestrians killed.

Jan 2023

10th & 11th St Bikeway completed: concrete curb-protected lanes, 14 transit islands, protected intersections.

Dec 2023

$12.9M SS4A grant awarded — includes E Santa Clara St at 11th St improvements at Naglee Park's western boundary.

2025

41 traffic deaths — lowest since 2012. $90M+ invested in 1,000+ safety projects since 2021.

2026

Speed cameras operational — 33 cameras in school zones and high-injury corridors under AB 645 pilot.

Looking Ahead

VTA Central Bikeway

10.8-mile Class IV separated bikeway along 10th/11th Streets with ~50 raised crosswalks and 20 protected intersections.

SS4A: Santa Clara & 11th

Pedestrian refuge island, sidewalk extension, bulb-out corner, signal modification, raised bikeway. Construction ~late 2027.

Speed Camera Pilot

33 cameras through 2032 under AB 645. Income-adjusted fines, no DMV points. Expected 20–25% injury crash reduction.

San Fernando Bikeway

Upgrading plastic bollards to permanent concrete islands with protected intersections. Construction began March 2026.
